Layout philosophy
One-screen register. Products left, cart right, customer chip top. No hidden nav, no submenus. An employee can ring up a soda in under 2 seconds: tap Coke → tap Charge → tap Card.
Data source
Tiles are driven by the existing services table where pricelist = 'Retail'. Each service has a pricing_type (per_lb / per_bag / per_item) and a new category (Laundry / Drinks / Snacks / Supplies) that maps to the 4 tabs.
Scale & weight
Per-lb services (Wash & Fold, Wash & Dry) open a big number pad. Employee reads the weight from the scale display and types it in. Live price calculation shows immediately so the customer sees the math before adding.
Add-ons (Oxi, Vinegar, Double Wash)
Add-ons live inside the weight modal, right below the numpad — one modal, one decision, no extra screen. Each is a flat per-drop-off charge ($3 Oxi, $3 Vinegar, $5 Double Wash) that travels with the specific W&F or W&D batch. The live total line above the numpad updates as the attendant toggles chips, so the customer sees the all-in price before anything is committed. In the cart, add-ons appear as small purple pills directly under the W&F line — visibly tied to the drop-off they belong to — and the pills will print as line items on the receipt. Tap the Weight + add-ons flow button above to see the modal mid-entry with Oxi and Vinegar already selected.
Customer chip
Defaults to "Walk-in" — the POS never blocks a sale. Tap the chip to open phone lookup. If the number is already in our system, one tap attaches them (name, history, credits). If not, we fall through to the new-customer flow — see next card.
New customer onboarding
When phone lookup returns no match, the attendant sees a + Create new customer button. The create form asks for first name, last name, and (optional) email — phone is pre-filled from the lookup. Saves in under 10 seconds, attaches to the current sale, creates a customers row with pricelist = 'Delivery'. A small on-screen disclosure notes that the number will be used for ready-for-pickup texts (implicit consent; transactional only, not marketing).
App invite (email only)
If the attendant captures an email during onboarding, we auto-queue a welcome email after the sale with a link to download the delivery app. No email = no invite. This keeps the counter experience fast while still giving loyal walk-ins a path into the full delivery service later — on their terms.
Payment
Two big buttons: Card (Stripe Terminal) and Cash. Card flow shows a waiting state with a pulsing animation while the reader processes. Cash flow (not fully drawn here) pops a quick "amount tendered" numpad and calculates change.
Receipt
After payment: three equal options — Print (CloudPRNT to the mC-Print3, same path as delivery receipts), Email, or Text. A walk-in with no customer attached only gets Print. Auto-advances back to a fresh register after 5 seconds.
